The 20th season of The Bachelor premiered on January 4, 2016.[1] This season features 26-year-old Ben Higgins, a software salesman from Warsaw, Indiana.

Higgins attended Indiana University where he was graduated with a BS in business administration and management through the school of Public and environmental affairs (SPEA). In addition, he was also a member of Delta Upsilon's Indiana chapter where he served as the vice president of external affairs. Higgins placed third on the 11th season of The Bachelorette featuring Kaitlyn Bristowe.

Filming and development[edit]

Casting and contestants[edit]

Casting began during the 19th season of the show. On August 24, 2015, during season one, episode four of Bachelor in Paradise: After Paradise, Ben Higgins was announced as the next Bachelor.[2][3]

The cast includes season 19 runner-up Becca Tilley and fellow contestant Amber James, news anchor Olivia Caridi from WCYB-TV and Joelle "JoJo" Fletcher, who is a half-sister of Ready for Love star Ben Patton.[4][5][6]

Production[edit]

The season traveled to many places including Las Vegas, Nevada, Mexico City, Mexico, Pig Island in The Bahamas and the state of Indiana.[citation needed] With appearances from rapper Ice Cube, comedian Kevin Hart, soccer players Alex Morgan, Kelley O'Hara, comedian Terry Fator, and Indiana Pacers basketball player Paul George.[7]

Future appearances[edit]

Caila Quinn was originally chosen to be the bachelorette for season twelve of The Bachelorette,[42] but the producers dropped her just days before the official announcement to Joelle "JoJo" Fletcher instead.[43] Quinn, Lauren Himle, Tiara Soleim, Jami Letain, Shushanna Mkrtychyan, Jennifer Saviano, Isabel "Izzy" Goodkind, Lace Morris, Jubilee Sharpe, Leah Block, Haley Ferguson, Emily Ferguson, and Amanda Stanton were chosen to compete for the third season of Bachelor in Paradise. Sharpe and Block were eliminated in 28th/29th. The Ferguson sisters quit in 22nd/23rd. Quinn quit in 17th/18th place, along with Bachelor alumnus, Jared Haibon. Goodkind quit in 16th place. Mkrtychyan quit in 15th place. Letain, Himle, and Soleim were eliminated in 11th/13th place. Saviano quit in 7th/8th place, alongside Nick Viall. Morris and Stanton finished as co-winners with Bachelor Nation alumni, Grant Kemp and Josh Murray.

203 10 "The Women Tell All" March 7, 2016 (2016-03-07) TBA 7.48[53]

Seventeen of the twenty-six eliminated contestants attended the "Women Tell All" special, sitting in this particular order: Lace, Tiara, Amber, Jami, Izzy, Rachel, Jubilee, Shushanna, Jennifer, Lauren H., Leah, Olivia, Becca, Haley, Emily, Amanda and Caila.

Jubilee was the first one in the hot seat and recalled about her experience on the show, including her breakdown, and later revealed that she was promoted to Sergeant in the United States Army. Next, Lace expresses her emotions during her first kiss with Ben, and one Bachelor fan comes onto the stage, taking off his shirt to reveal a tattoo of Lace on his chest. Chris then asks Lace to join the Bachelor in Paradise cast. Olivia is questioned as to why the other women despise her with Chris recalling a situation where Olivia calls Amanda a "Teen Mom". She states that she felt targeted, and revealed that she was bullied as a child. She gives Amanda a heartfelt apology. Then, second runner-up Caila comes on stage and explains her emotions when she told Ben she loved him and he didn't tell her he loved her back. She explains her heartbreak when Ben sends her home unexpectedly. Later, Ben comes in to see the ladies, talking specifically to Jubilee, Caila, Amanda and Olivia. Chris tests Ben to see if he can tell the twins apart. Ben finishes the show by stating that he is engaged to the winner and "would marry her tomorrow".

The bloopers were shown including one member of production crew falling in the pool during Ben and JoJo's overnight date, Olivia's confessional interview causing a power failure, and Becca's Bachelor season mix-up talking to other ladies.
204 11 "Season Finale: Week 10" March 14, 2016 (2016-03-14) 2010 9.58[54]

Both Lauren and JoJo have an opportunity to meet Ben's parents (David and Amy) on a villa in Jamaica where they have spent time with Ben. Although the parents praised Ben's strong feelings with the ladies, Ben remains undecided about his decision. Ben has one last final date with each of the two ladies. Lauren and Ben's final date involved them snuggling on a catamaran and they had a picnic on a beach. They spent time in Lauren's hotel room, Ben leaves at the end of the night a little worried that their relationship is too good to be true as it hasn't been tested. Ben's final date with JoJo involved swimming in the waterfalls. Just like Lauren, Ben came in JoJo's hotel room, where JoJo questioned Ben's feelings and Ben admitted that he is falling in love with both JoJo and Lauren, and JoJo ends up sobbing on her bathroom floor.

The day of the final rose ceremony, Neil Lane comes to the villa and presents Ben with a choice of engagement rings. During this time Ben is finally clear on who he wants to choose. JoJo arrived first to see Ben and started professing her love, stating how much she wanted to be with him and how in love with him she was. Ben tells JoJo that he did find love with her, but was more in love with Lauren. JoJo left feeling blind-sided, as Ben constantly told her he loved her and couldn't live without her. Before Lauren arrives to see Ben, he calls her dad and asks him for permission to take Lauren's hand in marriage and he gives Ben his blessing. Lauren arrives, they profess their love to each other and Ben kneels down on one knee and asks Lauren to marry him. She accepts and Ben asks, "Lauren, will you accept this rose?", with her answer being "Yes".
205 12 "After the Final Rose" March 14, 2016 (2016-03-14) TBA 9.24[54]

Earlier in the The Women Tell All special, Ben indicated that he would "marry her (the winner) tomorrow", so Chris Harrison arranged for both families and Ben's pastor to be on hand in case they wanted to marry on live TV. First, Ben came out and said that he was completely happy and was certain that he had made the right decision. Then, JoJo came out to face Ben for the first time since Jamaica. Ben apologized again for breaking her heart but he simply realized that he loved Lauren even more than her. JoJo said that she was very hurt at first, but after watching the season she saw the connection that Ben had with Lauren and was able to understand his decision. Chris Harrison announced that JoJo would be the next Bachelorette.

Then, Lauren came out and she and Ben continued to profess their strong love for each other during their first public appearance. They discuss their future plans, including an announcement that Lauren will be moving from Southern California (Marina del Rey) to Denver, Colorado to be with Ben. In addition to Ben's pastor and the families, Jimmy Kimmel appears in the audience as well, making jokes about their overnight date from Week 9. They decided not to get married during the show, but Ben proposed to Lauren once again in front of the studio audience with their families present. They have promised to marry soon.
